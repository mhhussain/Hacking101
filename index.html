Types of hackers

  • White hat
  • Black hat
  • Grey hat

White hat

Professional security specialists, primarily working to prevent malicious hacking.

  • Antivirus
  • Code auditing
  • Encryption
  • Network and system admins

Black hat

Personal gain and malicious intent.

  • Stealing information -
    CC, SSN, medical records, etc.
  • Damage a system
  • Disturb the peace

Grey hat

Somewhere in the middle? Hacking for the sake of hacking, but not really helping or hindering anyone.

Anonymity

Proxy chain

Proxy: Middle man. Requests information your behalf and parrots it back to you.

- Imagine you have a health insurance, but your friend Olly Oxenfree does not. - Routing calls through a bunch of different phones.

Tor & VPN

Tor

Similar to proxies, except far more advanced and secure. It encrypts all information including packet headers and sends information randomly from node to node. Each node then decrypts only the information that it needs to know where the packet came from and where it's going. Then reencrypts and sends it along.

Tor & VPN

VPN

Is a tunnel through a private server that all internet traffic travels through.

- The goons were your proxy/vpn and they bounced from car to car. And let's say you only told them the location of the first car, and each car had the location of the next until you get to the end.

Anonymity

macchanger

Media Access Control (MAC) Address. A unique identifier given to each device connected to a computer.

6C:70:9F:81:A7:C2

- First three number are manufacturer specific. The next three are unique to a machine. MAC addresses only ever make it as far as the router and are used to identify a specific machine. You can restrict access to a network by MAC address.

Anonymity & Infiltration

Infiltration

Footprinting and nmap

Gathering information about a computer system and all devices and systems that it can connect to.

- Reconnaissance. Allows you to map out a network. Ping sweeps, port scanning - Nmap is a program that allows you to do that. - Ransomware, web crawlers

Infiltration

DOS/DDOS and Signal Jamming

Denial of Service (DOS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDOS) is a technique of overloading a system by issuing a flurry of requests.

- There are many ways to prevent this, the easiest is to block the IP or MAC Address. A DDOS attack attempts to subvert that. - Also companies and programs that offer DOS and DDOS as a service for stress testing purposes.

Infiltration

Reverse shell

Remote Access Tool (RAT), forces a target machine to connect to you.

- Typically, you attempt to connect to a target machine, but if you cannot, you can open up a port on your system and have target machine connect to you and give you a remote shell. - Helps get around firewalls. Firewalls typically block access coming in, but rules for outgoing access is not as strict.
